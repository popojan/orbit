## Core Claim

The three main Giza pyramids have height/base ratios that are **consecutive convergents of √φ/2**:

| Pyramid | Height | Base | Ratio | Convergent # |
|---------|--------|------|-------|--------------|
| Menkaure | 125 | 200 | **5/8** | 3rd |
| Khafre | 274 | 411 | **2/3** | 2nd |
| Khufu | 280 | 440 | **7/11** | 4th |

Convergents of √φ/2: 1/2, **2/3**, **5/8**, **7/11**, 159/250, ...

**Chronology note:** Construction order (Khufu→Khafre→Menkaure) does not match convergent order (2nd→3rd→4th). The sequence is #4→#2→#3. Whether this is significant is unclear — Egyptians had no reason to expect future dating precision.

### Chronological Gap: Caveat or Advantage?

| Period | Date | Evidence |
|--------|------|----------|
| **Giza pyramids** | ~2550 BCE | 4th Dynasty |
| White Chapel (Senwosret I) | ~1950 BCE | 12th Dynasty, earliest setjat table |
| **Surviving cubit rods** | ~1350-850 BCE | 18th-22nd Dynasty |

**The gap:** Cubit rods are from **~1000 years AFTER** the pyramids!

**Caveat interpretation:**
- We extrapolate backwards assuming cubit didn't change
- No direct 4th Dynasty evidence for cubit length

**Advantage interpretation:**
- Later rods are **ceremonial copies** with degraded accuracy
- JAEA: *"they often incorporate mistakes, and the graduations are sometimes clumsily drawn"*
- Original construction standards may have been **more precise**
- Pyramid precision (0.1%) >> rod variation (1%) suggests lost master standard

**Supporting evidence:**
- Petrie derived cubit from pyramid → 523.7 mm
- This matches later rods (522-527 mm)
- Consistency across 1000+ years suggests stable tradition
- JAEA: *"There can be no doubt that there was one, or even several standard rods, which were closely guarded and better manufactured"*

**Conclusion:** The surviving rods may be **degraded ceremonial copies** of an older, more precise standard used to build the pyramids.

## The Seked Connection

Egyptian slope measure: **seked** = horizontal palms per 1 cubit rise

**Key:** 1 royal cubit = **7** palms

### All Three Giza Sekeds

| Pyramid | h/b ratio | Seked (palms) | Angle |
|---------|-----------|---------------|-------|
| Khafre | 2/3 | **5.25** | 53.13° |
| Khufu | 7/11 | **5.5** | 51.84° |
| Menkaure | 5/8 | **5.6** | 51.34° |

**Calculation:** seked = 7 × (base/height) / 2 = 7/(2r) where r = h/b

### Bracketing √φ/2

The three ratios **bracket** √φ/2 from both sides:

```
5/8 = 0.625 < √φ/2 ≈ 0.636 < 2/3 = 0.667
      ↑                ↑              ↑
   Menkaure         Khufu          Khafre
```

**Khufu (7/11 ≈ 0.6364)** is the closest simple fraction to √φ/2.

### Why 7 in the Numerator?

```
Khufu seked = 5.5 palms = 5p 2d = 22/28 cubits

Slope = 1 cubit rise / (22/28 cubit run) = 28/22 = 14/11

tan(θ) = 14/11 → h/b = 7/11
```

**Observation:** The numerator **7** in 7/11 comes directly from **7 palms per cubit**. The seked system naturally produces ratios with 7 in the numerator — no mystical knowledge required, just their measurement system.
