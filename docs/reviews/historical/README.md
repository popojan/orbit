# Historical Peer Reviews: An Anachronistic Thought Experiment

**What is this?** Imagined reviews of modern mathematical work (Egypt trajectory, Chebyshev polynomials, hyperbolic geometry connections) from the perspective of historical mathematicians who would have been most qualified to evaluate it.

**Why?** Educational demonstration of:
- How different mathematical perspectives illuminate different aspects
- Importance of historical accuracy in mathematical writing
- Standards of rigor across different mathematical traditions
- Fun way to check our work through multiple lenses

**Disclaimer:** These are **anachronistic thought experiments** - the historical figures could not have actually reviewed this work. All historical facts (dates, places, relationships, publications) have been verified against reliable sources. The reviews use period-appropriate language and focus on what each mathematician would genuinely have found interesting or problematic.
